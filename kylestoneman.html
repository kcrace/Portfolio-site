Kyle Stoneman

Cultural Historian

PhD Candidate, Univerity of Cambridge / KyleStoneman@mac.com

My work is inspired by historical revivals, humor, myth, interactions between text and image, and Anglo-American cultural exchange. I focus primarily on British art, but I am equally at home with American art of the nineteenth- and twentieth-centuries, as shown in the diverse range of my research topics: the Gothic revival in the American South, the iconography of King Arthur in America, and Bloomsbury painters in New Mexico. My current project explores the interwoven relations between the collecting habits, fictional writings, and visual output of interwar authors and artists such as Evelyn Waugh and Carl Van Vechten.
